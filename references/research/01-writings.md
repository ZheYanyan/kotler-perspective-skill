# Writings & Systematic Thinking

## Core Frameworks (appear in 3+ works)

### 1. The 4Ps Marketing Mix (Product, Price, Place, Promotion)
- **Origin**: Developed by Neil Borden (1953) and Jerome McCarthy (1960); Kotler codified and popularized it across his works
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management (all editions), Principles of Marketing, Kotler on Marketing
- **Definition**: A controllable set of marketing decisions companies use to affect demand
- **Evolution**: Expanded to 4As (Acceptability, Accessibility, Affordability, Awareness) in Marketing 4.0

### 2. Customer Delivered Value (Customer Value Framework)
- **Core concept**: Total Customer Value minus Total Customer Cost = Customer Delivered Value
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management, Principles of Marketing, Kotler on Marketing
- **Components**:
  - Product value, Services value, Personnel value, Image value
  - Monetary costs, Time costs, Energy costs, Psychic costs
- **Purpose**: Explains how customers make trade-off decisions

### 3. Strategic Planning & Portfolio Analysis (SBU Framework)
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management, Kotler on Marketing, Marketing Excellence
- **Components**: Boston Consulting Group (BCG) matrix, GE-McKinsey matrix
- **Steps**: Define corporate mission, Establish strategic business units (SBUs), Allocate resources, Assess growth opportunities

### 4. SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management, Strategic Planning guides, multiple marketing textbooks
- **Kotler's contribution**: Integrated SWOT into marketing planning process

### 5. TOWS Analysis (，倒SWOT)
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management (later editions), Strategic Marketing for Nonprofit Organizations
- **Difference from SWOT**: Analyzes external opportunities/threats first, then internal strengths/weaknesses

### 6. Marketing Evolution Framework (1.0 through 6.0)
- **Marketing 1.0** (Product-driven): "Product is what you make"
- **Marketing 2.0** (Consumer-driven): Differentiation and positioning
- **Marketing 3.0** (Value-driven): Human spirit, corporate social responsibility
- **Marketing 4.0** (Digital): Connected economy, online/offline integration
- **Marketing 5.0** (Technology): AI, big data, marketing automation
- **Marketing 6.0** (Immersive): Metaverse, Web3, experiential marketing

### 7. Holistic Marketing Concept
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management (recent editions), Marketing 3.0, Kotler on Marketing
- **Four pillars**:
  - Relationship marketing
  - Integrated marketing
  - Internal marketing
  - Performance marketing

### 8. 5A Customer Journey Framework
- **Aware**: Customer becomes aware of brand
- **Appeal**: Create distinctive brand appeal
- **Ask**: Encourage inquiry and research
- **Act**: Drive purchase behavior
- **Advocate**: Transform into brand advocates

### 9. Marketing Planning Process
- **Appears in**: All major marketing books
- **Steps**: Situation analysis, Objectives, Strategies, Plans, Implementation, Control

### 10. Market Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning (STP)
- **Appears in**: Marketing Management, Principles of Marketing, Kotler on Marketing
- **Process**: Divide market, Select target segments, Develop positioning

## Coined Terms

### Marketing Terms Introduced/Popularized by Kotler

1. **Social Marketing** - Using marketing principles to influence behavior change for social good (1970s)

2. **Demarketing** - Temporary or permanent reduction of demand through marketing techniques

3. **Megamarketing** - Using the 4 Ps plus power (public relations, lobbying, legal action) to gain access to markets

4. **Countertrade Marketing** - International marketing through barter and exchange mechanisms

5. **Holistic Marketing** - Comprehensive marketing approach combining all marketing functions

6. **Marketing Myopia** - Company's excessive focus on products rather than customer needs (repopulated from Levitt)

7. **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** - Present value of future cash flows from customer relationship

8. **Total Customer Value** - Aggregate benefits customers expect from a product

9. **Total Customer Cost** - Aggregate costs customers incur to obtain/ use a product

10. **Customer Delivered Value** - Gap between total customer value and total customer cost

11. **Marketing 3.0** - Value-driven marketing era

12. **Marketing 4.0** - Digital marketing era

13. **Marketing 5.0** - Technology-enabled marketing

14. **Marketing 6.0** - Immersive experiential marketing

15. **Lateral Marketing** (with Jean-Baptiste Siguenza) - Horizontal/divergent thinking applied to market creation

16. **Database Marketing** - Using customer databases for targeted communication

17. **Direct Marketing** - Direct communication with customers without intermediaries

18. **Key Account Management** - Managing strategic customer relationships

19. **Market Sensing** - Continuous intelligence gathering about markets

20. **Market Driving** - Influencing market structure and dynamics rather than just responding

21. **Retail Ken** - Understanding the complete retail environment and context

22. **Geographic Segmentation** - Dividing markets by geographic units

23. **Benefit Segmentation** - Dividing markets by product benefits sought

24. **Positioning** - Establishing brand identity in consumer mind

## Key Arguments

### 1. Marketing is Both Social and Managerial Process
> "Marketing is a societal and managerial process by which individuals and groups create, offer, and exchange products and services of value for满足 their needs and wants."

This dual definition, appearing consistently across all editions, represents Kotler's foundational belief that marketing serves both individual desires and broader social welfare.

### 2. Customer Value is Central
Kotler consistently argues that the company's primary task is to deliver customer value superior to competitors. All marketing decisions should revolve around:
- Understanding customer needs and wants
- Creating superior value proposition
- Delivering value through integrated marketing programs
- Managing long-term customer relationships

### 3. Strategic Marketing Planning Must Precede Operational Marketing
Kotler insists on the hierarchy:
1. Corporate/strategic planning sets direction
2. Business unit planning defines competitive positioning
3. Marketing planning implements tactical programs

### 4. Marketing Drives Economic Growth
- Strong marketing sector indicates strong economy
- Marketing determines what products succeed in the marketplace
- Marketing creates utility (form, time, place, possession)

### 5. Companies Must Balance Profit and Social Responsibility
- Corporate social responsibility (CSR) is not optional
- Social marketing improves quality of life
- Companies must pursue ethical practices and sustainable development

### 6. Professional vs. Entrepreneurial Marketing
Kotler distinguishes:
- **Professional Marketers**: Formally trained, systematic approach, may miss opportunities
- **Entrepreneurial Marketers**: Opportunity-seeking, intuitive, may lack discipline
- Companies need both orientations

### 7. The Future is Value-Driven and Human-Centered
From Marketing 3.0 onward, Kotler argues:
- Consumers seek meaning and purpose, not just transactions
- Companies must stand for something beyond profits
- Technology should enhance human connection, not replace it

### 8. Marketing is Too Important to Be Left to Marketing Departments
- Every employee is a "marketing ambassador"
- Internal marketing must precede external marketing
- Marketing integration across all functions is essential

### 9. Companies Cannot Afford Marketing Myopia
- Focus on customer needs, not just product features
- Continuously redefine the business definition
- Think in terms of customer value categories

### 10. Marketing Metrics Must Include Non-Financial Dimensions
- Customer satisfaction and loyalty
- Brand equity
- Market share trends
- Social impact measures
- Customer acquisition and retention costs
